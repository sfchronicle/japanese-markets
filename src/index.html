How sea urchin from Tsukiji fish market comes to San Francisco

1. In Hokkaido, Japan, fishermen catch sea urchin (uni). Local company Hamanaka Ogawa buys the sea urchin from the fishermen, cracks open the shells, scoops out the meat and places it in seawater to prevent spoiling.

2. Sea urchin packages are flown to Tokyo and brought to Tsukiji Market.

3. The next morning at around 5 a.m., Chuo Gyorui auctions the sea urchin, selling it for a rate of 1,000 to 5,000 Japanese yen ($9 to $46) for 100 grams (about 3.5 ounces). In general, the sea urchin is sold two days after it is killed.

4. Japanese seafood buyer Sakasyu, which operates out of Tsukiji Market, buys the sea urchin from the auction and then packs it, and other seafood, to send to San Francisco later that morning.

5. Bay Area seafood wholesaler ABS picks up the seafood package at San Francisco International Airport for Ju-Ni restaurant and stores it in a San Francisco warehouse later that afternoon. The restaurant receives sea urchin every day. In general, once it arrives at the restaurant, high-quality uni lasts three days.
